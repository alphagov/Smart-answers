The husband, wife or civil partner gets the house up to a value of £473,000. They’ll get a lump sum of £473,000 if the house is worth more, and may have to sell off the property.{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>They also get:</p>

<ul>
  <li>up to £89,000 in cash</li>
  <li>a third of the rest of the estate (or the entire estate if the cash sum was less than £89,000)</li>
</ul>

<p>The rest of the estate is divided in half between any surviving:</p>

<ul>
  <li>parents</li>
  <li>brothers or sisters</li>
</ul>

<p>If a brother or sister has already died, their children (nieces or nephews of the deceased) inherit in their place.</p>
