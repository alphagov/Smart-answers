<h2 id="statutory-paternity-leave">Statutory Paternity Leave</h2>

<p>The employee is entitled to Statutory Paternity Leave.</p>

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>Leave</th>
      <th>Key dates</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>Start</td>
      <td>1 January 2015</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>End</td>
      <td>8 January 2015</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Latest date to <a href="/employers-paternity-pay-leave/adoption">claim leave</a></td>
      <td>20 September 2014</td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>Leave must finish within 56 days of the birth (or due date if the baby is early).</p>
</div>

<h2 id="statutory-paternity-pay">Statutory Paternity Pay</h2>

<p>The employee is not entitled to Statutory Paternity Pay. To qualify:</p>

<ul>
  <li>they must still be employed by you on Thursday, 01 January 2015</li>
</ul>

<p>Send them form OSPP1 confirming this within 28 days of their pay request.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.hmrc.gov.uk/forms/spp1.pdf">Download Form OSPP1, refusing Statutory Paternity Pay (PDF, 100KB)</a></p>
</div>
