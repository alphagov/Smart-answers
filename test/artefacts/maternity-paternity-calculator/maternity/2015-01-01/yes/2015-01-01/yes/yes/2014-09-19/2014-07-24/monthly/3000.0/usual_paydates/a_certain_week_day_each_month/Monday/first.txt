<h2 id="statutory-maternity-pay">Statutory Maternity Pay</h2>

<p>The employee is entitled to up to 39 weeks Statutory Maternity Pay (<abbr title="Statutory Maternity Pay">SMP</abbr>) if they <a href="/maternity-leave-pay-employees/notice-period" title="Notice period">claim <abbr title="Statutory Maternity Pay">SMP</abbr> in time</a> and give <a href="/maternity-leave-pay-employees/eligibility" title="Proof of pregnancy">proof of the pregnancy</a>.</p>

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>Pay</th>
      <th>Key facts</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>Average weekly earnings</td>
      <td>£346.15385 (don&rsquo;t round this up or down)</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Latest date to claim <abbr title="Statutory Maternity Pay">SMP</abbr></td>
      <td>4 December 2014</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><abbr title="Statutory Maternity Pay">SMP</abbr> start date due to pregnancy related illness</td>
      <td>30 November 2014</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Proof of pregnancy</td>
      <td>Usually within 21 days of the <abbr title="Statutory Maternity Pay">SMP</abbr> start date (or as soon as possible if baby born early)</td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
