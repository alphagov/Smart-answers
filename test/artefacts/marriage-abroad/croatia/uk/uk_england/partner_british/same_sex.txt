<h2 id="what-documents-youll-need">What documents you’ll need</h2>

<p>You’ll need proof that you’ve been resident in the area covered by the British embassy or consulate for at least 7 days, like an employer’s letter or a bank statement.</p>

<p>You’ll both need your original passports. If either of you have been divorced, widowed or in a civil partnership before, you’ll also need:</p>

<ul>
  <li>a <a href="/copy-decree-absolute-final-order">decree absolute or final order</a>, or</li>
  <li>the <a href="/order-copy-birth-death-marriage-certificate/">death certificate</a></li>
</ul>

<h3 id="what-you-need-to-do">What you need to do</h3>

<p>Once you’ve made your appointment, the British embassy or consulate will give you:</p>

<ul>
  <li>a notice of registration</li>
  <li>a declaration that you and your partner will need to swear, stating that you’re legally entitled to enter into a civil partnership</li>
</ul>

<p>Once you’ve submitted these and paid the registration fee (see below), the British embassy or consulate will display your notice publicly for 14 days.</p>

<p>As long as nobody has registered an objection after this time, the registration officer can then register your partnership any time until 3 months after the date you gave notice.</p>

<p>You’ll need to provide two witnesses and pay a fee to register your civil partnership. You’ll need to pay an additional fee for your civil partnership certificate (see below).</p>
