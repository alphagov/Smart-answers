Marriage in Germany{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Germany to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="what-you-need-to-do">What you need to do</h2>

<p>You don’t need a certificate of no impediment (<abbr title="certificate of no impediment">CNI</abbr>) to prove you’re allowed to marry if you’re a British national resident outside of the UK. However, you’ll need to apply for an exemption (‘Befreiung von der Beibringung des Ehefähigkeitszeugnisses’).</p>

<p>The local registry office (‘Standesamt’) where you’re getting married should tell you what to do.</p>

<p>If the Standesamt does ask you for a <abbr title="certificate of no impediment">CNI</abbr> (‘Ehefähigkeitszeugnis’), refer them to the relevant part of the <a rel="external" href="http://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bgb/__1309.html">German Civil Code</a> (‘Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch’). This is published on the Stuttgart Higher Regional Court (‘Oberlandesgericht’) website, but it applies to the whole of Germany.</p>

<p>Check with the Higher Regional Court where you’re getting married if they don’t accept this.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You don&rsquo;t need to stay in the country while your notice is posted.</p>
</div>
