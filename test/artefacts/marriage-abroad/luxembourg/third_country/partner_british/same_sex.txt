Civil partnership in Luxembourg{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Same-sex relationships in Luxembourg that are recognised as civil partnerships under British law are known as ’partenariat enregistré’, or ’eingetragene partnerschaft’ (’registered partnership’).</p>

<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Luxembourg to find out about local laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<p>You should also check the <a href="/foreign-travel-advice/luxembourg">travel advice for Luxembourg</a>.</p>

<h2 id="what-you-need-to-do">What you need to do</h2>

<p>You may be asked to provide a certificate of no impediment (CNI) or a similar document to prove you’re allowed to enter into a civil partnership or equivalent in Luxembourg.</p>

<p>Contact the local British embassy or consulate where you’re planning the ceremony to find out what you need to do.</p>
