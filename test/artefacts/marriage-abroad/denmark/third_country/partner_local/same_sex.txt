Civil partnership in Denmark{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Same-sex relationships in Denmark that are recognised as civil partnerships under British law are known as:</p>

<ul>
  <li>marriage</li>
  <li>’registreret partnerskab’ (’registered partnership’)</li>
  <li>‘nalunaarsukkamik inooqatigiinneq’ (in Greenland)</li>
</ul>

<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Denmark to find out about local laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<p>You should also check the <a href="/foreign-travel-advice/denmark">travel advice for Denmark</a>.</p>

<h2 id="what-you-need-to-do">What you need to do</h2>

<p>You may be asked to provide a certificate of no impediment (CNI) or a similar document to prove you’re allowed to enter into a civil partnership or equivalent in Denmark.</p>

<p>Contact the local British embassy or consulate where you’re planning the ceremony to find out what you need to do.</p>

<h2 id="naturalisation-of-your-partner-if-they-move-to-the-uk">Naturalisation of your partner if they move to the UK</h2>

<p>Your partner can apply to <a href="/becoming-a-british-citizen/if-your-spouse-is-a-british-citizen">become a British citizen</a> once they’ve lived in the UK for 3 years.</p>
