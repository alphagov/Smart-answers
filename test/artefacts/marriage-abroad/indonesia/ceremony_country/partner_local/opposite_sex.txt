Marriage in Indonesia
{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Make an appointment at the local British embassy or consulate in Indonesia to swear an affidavit (written statement of facts) that you’re free to marry.</p>

<p>Before the appointment you’ll need to pay a fee to the British Embassy in Jakarta. You can do this by credit card or in cash in the local currency. You also need to complete 2 copies of the <a href="/government/publications/marriage-in-indonesia">‘Affidavit for marriage’ form</a>.</p>

<p>Send one copy to the British Embassy in Jakarta by email or post. Take the other one with you when you go to your local appointment, along with your passport.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>Allow the embassy at least 2 days to process your paperwork before turning up for your appointment.</p>
</div>

<p>If you’ve been divorced or widowed, you’ll need to provide:</p>

<ul>
  <li>a <a href="/copy-decree-absolute-final-order">decree absolute or final order</a> or <a href="/order-copy-birth-death-marriage-certificate/">death certificate</a></li>
  <li>evidence of nationality or residence where your divorce took place if it was outside the UK. You’ll need to get it <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">translated</a> if it’s not in English</li>
  <li>evidence if you’ve changed your name by deed poll</li>
</ul>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>Your partner will need to get an affidavit as well.</p>
</div>
