Marriage in Aruba{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Aruba is one of the Dutch Caribbean islands.</p>

<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Aruba to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> and check the <a href="/foreign-travel-advice/aruba">travel advice for Aruba</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>

<p>There aren’t any British consular facilities to register a marriage or get legal documents to prove you’re eligible to marry in Aruba.</p>

<h2 id="naturalisation-of-your-partner-if-they-move-to-the-uk">Naturalisation of your partner if they move to the UK</h2>

<p>Your partner can apply to <a href="/becoming-a-british-citizen/if-your-spouse-is-a-british-citizen">become a British citizen</a> once they’ve lived in the UK for 3 years.</p>
