Civil Partnership in Canada{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Same-sex relationships in Canada that are recognised as civil partnerships under British law are known as:</p>

<ul>
  <li>marriage</li>
  <li>’union de fait’, or ’common-law relationship’ (in Manitoba)</li>
  <li>’domestic partnership’ (in Nova Scotia)</li>
  <li>’union civile’, or ’civil union’ (in Quebec)</li>
</ul>

<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Canada to find out about local laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> and check the <a href="/foreign-travel-advice/canada">travel advice for Canada</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>
