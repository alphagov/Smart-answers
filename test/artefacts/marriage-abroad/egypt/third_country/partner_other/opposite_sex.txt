Marriage in Egypt

Contact the relevant local authorities in Egypt to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.


^You should [get legal advice](/government/collections/list-of-lawyers) and check the [travel advice for Egypt](/foreign-travel-advice/egypt) before making any plans.


##What you need to do


Make an appointment at the local British embassy or consulate in Egypt to make a declaration that you’re free to marry. You’ll need to bring your passport and pay a fee.

If you’ve been divorced or widowed, you’ll also need:

- a [decree absolute or final order](/copy-decree-absolute-final-order) or the [death certificate](/order-copy-birth-death-marriage-certificate/)


You’ll also need to provide evidence if you’ve changed your name by deed poll.


^Your partner will probably need to get an equivalent document from their national authorities.^


##Fees

Service | Fee
-|-
Marriage declaration | £45
Making an oath | £55


You normally have to pay fees for consular services in the local currency - these are shown in the list of [consular fees for Egypt](/government/publications/egypt-consular-fees).


You can pay by cash or credit card, but not by personal cheque.
