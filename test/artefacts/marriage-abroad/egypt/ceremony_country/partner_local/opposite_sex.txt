Marriage in Egypt{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Egypt to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="what-you-need-to-do">What you need to do</h2>

<p>Make an appointment at the local British embassy or consulate in Egypt to make a declaration that you’re free to marry. You’ll need to bring your passport and pay a fee.</p>

<div class="address"><div class="adr org fn"><p>
British Embassy Cairo, main contact<br />British Embassy<br />7 Ahmed Ragheb Street<br /> Garden City<br />Egypt<br />
</p></div></div>

<div class="contact">
<p>Telephone: +2 (02) 27916000<br />
Fax trade &amp; investment: +2 (02) 2791 6130
Fax political: +2 (02) 2791 6131
Fax Press: +2 (02) 2791 6132
Fax consular: +2 (02) 2791 6133</p>
</div>

<p>If you’ve been divorced or widowed, you’ll also need:</p>

<ul>
  <li>a <a href="/copy-decree-absolute-final-order">decree absolute or final order</a> or the <a href="/order-copy-birth-death-marriage-certificate/">death certificate</a></li>
</ul>

<p>You’ll also need to provide evidence if you’ve changed your name by deed poll.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>Your partner will probably need to get an equivalent document from their national authorities.</p>
</div>

<p>You normally have to pay fees for consular services in the local currency - these are shown in the list of <a href="/government/publications/egypt-consular-fees">consular fees for Egypt</a>.</p>

<p>You can pay by cash or credit card, but not by personal cheque.</p>
