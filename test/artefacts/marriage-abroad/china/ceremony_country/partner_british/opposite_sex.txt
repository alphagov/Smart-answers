Marriage in China{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in China to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="what-you-need-to-do">What you need to do</h2>

<p>Make an appointment at your local British embassy or consulate in China to swear an affirmation/affadavit (written statement of facts) that you’re free to marry. You’ll need to bring your passport and pay a fee.</p>

<p>You’ll need to complete an <a href="/government/publications/affirmation-form-china">‘Affirmation for marriage’</a> (non-religious) form or an <a href="/government/publications/affidavit-form-china">‘Affidavit for marriage’</a> (religious) form in English.</p>

<div class="address"><div class="adr org fn"><p>
British Embassy Beijing<br />11 Guang Hua Lu, Jian Guo Men Wai, Beijing<br />100600  <br />China<br />
</p></div></div>

<div class="contact">
<p>Telephone (General enquiries only): +86 0(10) 5192 4000 
Telephone (Consular assistance for British nationals): +86 0(10) 8529 6083
Fax (General): +86 0(10) 5192 4239
Fax (Trade &amp; Investment): +86 0(10) 5192 4218
Fax (Consular): +86 0(10) 8529 6081</p>
</div>

<p>If you’ve been divorced or widowed, you’ll need to provide:</p>

<ul>
  <li>a <a href="/copy-decree-absolute-final-order">decree absolute or final order</a> or <a href="/order-copy-birth-death-marriage-certificate/">death certificate</a></li>
  <li>evidence of nationality or residence where your divorce took place if it was outside the UK. You’ll need to get it <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">translated</a> if it’s not in English</li>
</ul>

<p>You’ll also need to provide evidence if you’ve changed your name by deed poll.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>Your partner will probably need to get an affirmation as well.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="fees">Fees</h2>

  <thead>
    <tr>

      <th>Fee</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>Affirmation for freedom to marry in English</td>
      <td>£45</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Affirmation for freedom to marry in any other language</td>
      <td>£70</td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

<p>You normally have to pay fees for consular services in the local currency - these are shown in the list of <a href="/government/publications/china-consular-fees">consular fees for China</a>.</p>

<p>You can pay by cash or credit card, but not by personal cheque.</p>
