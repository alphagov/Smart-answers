Marriage in Morocco{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Morocco to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="what-you-need-to-do">What you need to do</h2>

<p>You may be asked to provide an ‘affirmation for marriage’ document to prove you’re allowed to marry.</p>

<p>Make an appointment at the local British embassy or consulate in Morocco to swear an affidavit (written statement of facts) that you’re free to marry. You’ll need to bring your passport and pay a fee.</p>

<h3 id="legalisation-and-translation">Legalisation and translation</h3>

<p>You might need to get your affirmation <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">translated</a> into the local language and ‘<a href="/get-document-legalised">legalised</a>’ (certified as genuine) by the local authorities - the embassy or consulate should be able to give you advice.</p>

<p>If you’ve been divorced or widowed, you’ll need to provide:</p>

<ul>
  <li>a <a href="/copy-decree-absolute-final-order">decree absolute or final order</a> or <a href="/order-copy-birth-death-marriage-certificate/">death certificate</a></li>
  <li>evidence of nationality or residence where your divorce took place if it was outside the UK. You’ll need to get it <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">translated</a> if it’s not in English</li>
  <li>evidence if you’ve changed your name by deed poll</li>
</ul>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>An ’affirmation for marriage’ document issued in Morocco is valid for 3 months.</p>
</div>

<p>Your partner will probably need to get an equivalent document from their national authorities.</p>
