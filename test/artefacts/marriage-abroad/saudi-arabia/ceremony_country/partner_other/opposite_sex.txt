Marriage in Saudi Arabia{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>One of you must be a Muslim to get married under local laws in Saudi Arabia. Contact the <a rel="external" href="http://www.saudiembassy.org.uk/">Embassy of Saudi Arabia</a> to find out about local marriage laws.</p>

<h2 id="getting-married-at-the-british-embassy">Getting married at the British Embassy</h2>

<p>You can only apply to get married at the British Embassy in Riyadh if you’re resident in Saudi Arabia and neither of you is a Muslim. Contact them to make an appointment.</p>

<p>You’ll need to provide:</p>

<ul>
  <li>proof that you’ve been living in the country where you intend to get married for at least 21 days, e.g. an employer’s letter or a bank statement (you’ll also need to sign a declaration to confirm this)</li>
  <li>your original passport and <a href="/order-copy-birth-death-marriage-certificate">full birth certificate</a> or <a href="/get-replacement-citizenship-certificate">naturalisation certificate</a></li>
  <li>proof that you’re both over the age of 16, not within prohibited degrees of relationship to each other, and there’s no other impediment to your proposed marriage</li>
  <li>equivalent document for your partner</li>
</ul>

<p>If either of you have been divorced, widowed or in a civil partnership before, you’ll also need:</p>

<ul>
  <li>a <a href="/copy-decree-absolute-final-order">decree absolute or final order</a>, or</li>
  <li>the <a href="/order-copy-birth-death-marriage-certificate/">death certificate</a></li>
</ul>

<h3 id="what-happens-next">What happens next</h3>

<p>Once you’ve submitted these and paid the registration fee (see below), the embassy or consulate will post your marriage notice publicly for 14 days.</p>

<p>As long as nobody has registered an objection after this time, the consular officer can then perform the marriage ceremony any time until 3 months after the date you gave notice.</p>

<p>You’ll need to pay additional fees for the marriage ceremony and to get your marriage certificate (see below).</p>

<h2 id="naturalisation-of-your-partner-if-they-move-to-the-uk">Naturalisation of your partner if they move to the UK</h2>

<p>Your partner can apply to <a href="/becoming-a-british-citizen/if-your-spouse-is-a-british-citizen">become a British citizen</a> once they’ve lived in the UK for 3 years.</p>
