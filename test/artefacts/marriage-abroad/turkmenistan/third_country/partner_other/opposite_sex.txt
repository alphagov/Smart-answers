Marriage in Turkmenistan{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Turkmenistan to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> and check the <a href="/foreign-travel-advice/turkmenistan">travel advice for Turkmenistan</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="what-you-need-to-do">What you need to do</h2>

<p>You may be asked to provide a certificate of no impediment (<abbr title="Certificate of no impediment">CNI</abbr>) or a similar document to prove you’re allowed to marry.</p>

<p>There are 2 ways you can get a <abbr title="Certificate of no impediment">CNI</abbr>:</p>

<ul>
  <li><a href="/marriage-abroad/y/turkmenistan/uk/uk_england/partner_other/opposite_sex">go to the UK and post notice with a UK registrar</a></li>
  <li><a href="/marriage-abroad/y/turkmenistan/ceremony_country/partner_other/opposite_sex">go to Turkmenistan and post notice at the embassy or consulate there</a></li>
</ul>

<div role="note" aria-label="Help" class="application-notice help-notice">
<p>If you choose to post notice in Turkmenistan, you’ll normally have to wait at least 10 days before you can get married.</p>
</div>
