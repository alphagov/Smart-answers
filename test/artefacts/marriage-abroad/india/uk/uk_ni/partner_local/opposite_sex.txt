Marriage in India

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Contact the <a href="/government/publications/foreign-embassies-in-the-uk">High Commission of India</a> to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> and check the <a href="/foreign-travel-advice/india">travel advice for India</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>

<p>The UK doesn’t issue certificates of no impediment (<abbr title="certificate of no impediment">CNI</abbr>) for marriages in Commonwealth countries. You’ll need to explain this if you’re asked to provide a <abbr title="certificate of no impediment">CNI</abbr> or a similar document to prove you’re allowed to marry.</p>

<p>The British High Commission in India can provide you with a letter explaining this position for a charge - see <a href="/government/publications/india-consular-fees">consular fees for India</a>.</p>
