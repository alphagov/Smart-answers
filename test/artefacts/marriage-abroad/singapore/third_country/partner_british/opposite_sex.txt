Marriage in Singapore
{::comment}end-title{::/comment}

{::comment}begin-body{::/comment}
<p>Contact the relevant local authorities in Singapore to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
<p>You should <a href="/government/collections/list-of-lawyers">get legal advice</a> and check the <a href="/foreign-travel-advice/singapore">travel advice for Singapore</a> before making any plans.</p>
</div>

<p>The UK doesn’t issue certificates of no impediment (<abbr title="certificate of no impediment">CNI</abbr>) for marriages in Commonwealth countries. You’ll need to explain this if you’re asked to provide a <abbr title="certificate of no impediment">CNI</abbr> or a similar document to prove you’re allowed to marry.</p>

<p>However, you can swear an affidavit (written statement of facts) that you’re free to marry in front of a local <a rel="external" href="http://www.conp.sg/co-np-directory-listing">notary public or commissioner of oaths</a>.</p>
